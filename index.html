Why Should Kids Learn A New Language?

  • Improved Memory

    Learning a foreign language involves memorizing vocabulary and grammar, which enhances overall memory function. According to a study published in the journal "Neuropsychology," bilingualism has been linked to better memory performance (Bialystok et al., 2004).

  • Problem-Solving Skills

    Language learning stimulates critical thinking and problem-solving abilities. Research published in the journal "Psychological Science" suggests that bilingual individuals excel in tasks that require cognitive control and problem-solving (Green & Abutalebi, 2013).

  • Increased Creativity

    Language learners often find creative ways to express themselves. Studies, such as one published in "Frontiers in Psychology," have shown that bilingualism is associated with greater cognitive flexibility and divergent thinking, key components of creativity (Kharkhurin, 2012).

  • Learn To Overcome Obstacles

    Learning a new language presents a unique set of challenges, from understanding complex grammar rules to acquiring a new vocabulary. As children navigate these challenges, they develop resilience and learn to approach problems from various angles, skills that are invaluable in overcoming obstacles in other areas of life.

  • Discipline

    Consistency is key in language learning, requiring a disciplined approach to study and practice. Children who engage in regular language learning activities develop a sense of discipline that can translate into improved focus and dedication in their academic pursuits and daily routines.

Why start early?

  • Kids get the opportunity to explore

    Do you (parents) still remember the foreign language you learnt years ago at school or at university. Chances are you won't remember much of it. Starting early gives children to explore their interests.

  • Children are just better at learning stuff

    Children's brains are like sponges, absorbing new information much more rapidly than adults. This natural ability to learn, combined with their innate curiosity and willingness to experiment with new sounds and words, makes them exceptional language learners at an early age.

  • Longer time to learn a language

    The usual pathway to study in a university overseas will be to study a language intensively for 1-2 years after SPM. Starting early gives gives them the opportunity to learn at a slower pace and enjoy the process more than just cramming things in your head to pass that next exam.

  • Save costs

    Pre university foreign language courses can be quite heavy for students to bear in a short space of time. We help reduce these costs greatly and help parents space out the costs, so it will not feel as much a burden.
